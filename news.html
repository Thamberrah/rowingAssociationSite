High Performance Arm of British Rowing

This great british rowing club represents players for international competitions at both junior and senior levels. These competitions range through World Cups and World Championships to both Olympic and Paralympic Games.

Highlights

Highlight 1# of the decade:
Team GB's first gold at London 2012 Olympics for Glover and Stanning

Topping our highlights of the decade are Helen Glover and Heather Stanning. Olympic gold medals in the women's pair at London 2012.

Highlight 2# of the decade:
Andrew Triggs Hodge and Pete Reed become triple Olympic medalists

No 2# in our highlights of the decade: Andrew Triggs Hodge and Pete Reed won Olympic gold with the men's eight at the 2016 Rio Games.

Highlight 3# of the decade:
Great Britain finishes top at the Rio 2016 Paralympic Games

Britain Paralympic rowers brought home medals in every boat class at Rio, including three sensational golds, topping the table at the 2016 Games.

Highlight 4# of the decade:
Record crowds at Dorney for 2013 Samsung World Cup

A full house roared the GB Rowing Team on to more golden performances in 2013 on the Olympic Regatta course at Eton Dorney.

Highlight 5# of the decade:
Women’s Boat Race joins the men on the Tideway

Saturday 11 April 2015 was the day that the Oxford and Cambridge women’s crews made history when they raced on the Tideway.

Highlight 6# of the decade:
Grassroots rowing goes from strength to strength

With 76 new clubs, near-record entries across all competition formats from juniors to masters and BUCS to BRIC, rowing is growing.

Highlight 7# of the decade:
Moe Sbihi smashes 2km record at BRIC 2015

Number seven in our highlights of the decade is Moe Sbihi smashing Sir Matthew Pinsent’s 2km record – unbroken for 11 years until BRIC 2015.

Highlight 8# of the decade:
U23 rowers’ gold rush at Sarasota

Britain’s U23 rowers gave a dazzling display at the U23 World Rowing Championships topping the medal table with six golds and two silvers.
